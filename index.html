    About me

    Hey there! I'm Punam Woosaree, a computing science student at the University of Alberta in her final year. I chose Computer Science as my major after having taken various classes, could not find a path that both satisfied the logical and creative side of me. Computing Science provides the structure when building yet allows for creativity and exploration when designing. Since choosing this path I've met great minds at events like Hackathons and a great group of women involved in STEM activities from organizations like WISEST and student groups like Ada's Team. When I'm not struggling over assignments or overloading on coffee you can find me with my husky pup, hanging with friends or taking sub-par pictures. Thanks for listening!

    HackED 2019
    Attended HackED 2019 at the University of Alberta. Hackathons are a great opportunity to learn a skill or build a project in a timed environment with lots of people available to help. My friends and I decided to learn Swift to make our first IOS app. This app lets you list what tasks you have to do and then randomly picks one for you to do. Simple, but good learning experience!
    Task Away!
    An android app designed to pair users with tasks (tasks requesters) with users that can complete tasks (task providers). Task providers can bid on a task they wish to complete and Task requesters can select the winner of the bid. The app provides notifications for bids placed on a task or when a task is removed. Completed as part of CMPUT 301 at the University of Alberta.
    Industry Bear
    This Web App aims to connect industry mentors with UAlberta students so they can learn valuable industry skills. Industry has shown extreme interest in connecting and increasing their presence on the university campus. Students may be too scared to approach mentors and mentors might not know how to connect. This is where Industry Bear aids in bridging this gap. Stay tuned for more progress and exciting news!
