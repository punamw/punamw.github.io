    About me

    Hey there! I'm Punam Woosaree, a recent computing science graduate of the University of Alberta.

    My main passions are designing and implementing elegant and comprehensive systems, whether that be through UI/UX practices or design patterns. I love working and collaborating as a team, where everyones unique experience can be utilized together to create applications and solve world problems. Also, I enjoy a good struggle, and I'm never tired of finding new and efficient ways to solve a problem.

    In my past I've had the opportunity to work with many technologies, including but not limited to React, Meteor, Android, IOS, and MongoDB. While working on various applications I've used workflow management tools such as Git, Bitbucket, JIRA and Trello. Also, I have followed many design and software practices to ensure that all work and work flow is optimized and proceeding in good fashion.

    Choosing this path has allowed me to meet great minds at events like Hackathons and a great group of women involved in STEM activities from organizations like WISEST and student groups like Ada's Team. This field never ceases to amaze me in the opportunities and avenues of innovation that are constantly arising.

    Portfolio

    Check out more projects on my Github!
    HackED
    Attended HackED 2019 at the University of Alberta. Hackathons are a great opportunity to learn a skill or build a project in a timed environment with lots of people available to help. My friends and I decided to learn Swift to make our first IOS app. This app lets you list what tasks you have to do and then randomly picks one for you to do. Simple, but good learning experience!
    Walk and Talk
    A secure mobile application that brings together women in menopause transition or postmenopausal who are interested to try walking as a means to socialize and to alleviate their symptoms. It aims to support clinical research studies conducted by Mature Women’s Health researchers on menopause. Completed as part of CMPUT 401 at the University of Alberta.
    Industry Bear
    This Web App aims to connect industry mentors with UAlberta students so they can learn valuable industry skills. Industry has shown extreme interest in connecting and increasing their presence on the university campus. Students may be too scared to approach mentors and mentors might not know how to connect. This is where Industry Bear aids in bridging this gap.
